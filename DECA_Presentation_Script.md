### SLIDE 5 (Overview - What is PEST-Hub)

**GAVIN:** So what is PEST-Hub? At its core, PEST-Hub is an AI-powered mobile application that lets anyone identify agricultural pests instantly using just their smartphone camera. But it's much more than a simple identification app. What sets PEST-Hub apart is that it doesn't just recognize pests from a fixed list. It uses adaptive artificial intelligence to identify any pest, including species that have never been documented in our system before, and then it generates a complete information profile for that pest on the spot. That includes what the pest is, what kind of damage it causes, and exactly how to treat and prevent it, with both organic and chemical options.

The platform is designed from the ground up for real-world agricultural use. Everything happens on the farmer's smartphone, right there in the field. There's no special equipment to buy, no samples to send to a lab, no waiting days or weeks for results. You take a photo, and within seconds you have an answer you can act on.

We built PEST-Hub around four core components. The first is instant AI pest detection, where our system identifies a pest from a single photo in seconds and gives a confidence score so the user knows how certain the identification is. The second is our adaptive AI generation, which is our real innovation. When our system encounters a pest it hasn't seen before, instead of just saying "unknown" like every other tool on the market, our AI generates a full pest profile from scratch, covering the species description, damage symptoms, treatment options, and prevention strategies. The third component is dual treatment recommendations, meaning we provide both organic and chemical treatment paths for every pest because we know that different farmers have different practices and values. And the fourth is our mobile-first design. Everything is built for someone standing in a field at dawn, not someone sitting at a desk.

### SLIDE 8 (Problem - Barriers Farmers Face)

**GAVIN:** So why can't farmers just identify the pest and deal with it? Because the barriers are enormous.

First, delayed pest identification leads to rapid crop damage. Time is literally money in agriculture. Every hour a farmer spends trying to figure out what pest they're dealing with is another hour that infestation is spreading.

Second, there's extremely limited access to agricultural experts in rural areas. Most farmers don't have an entomologist on speed dial. Many are in remote locations where the nearest expert could be hours away, and getting an appointment might take days or even weeks. When you discover a pest problem at seven PM on a Saturday in rural Missouri, there is no one to call.

Third, misidentification results in ineffective or even harmful treatments. Using the wrong pesticide doesn't just waste money. It can actually make the infestation worse and harm beneficial insects that were helping protect the crops.

Fourth, small and mid-size farmers lack affordable diagnostic tools. Professional pest identification services are expensive. They often require physically mailing samples to a lab and then waiting for results. Small farmers simply cannot afford that, and they can't afford the time it takes either.

And fifth, new and region-specific pests are increasingly difficult to recognize. Climate change is actively shifting pest populations into new geographic areas. A farmer in the Midwest might suddenly encounter a pest species that was never a problem in their region before. They've never seen it, their neighbors have never seen it, and their usual references don't cover it.

### SLIDE 15 (Solution - Detection Flow)

**GAVIN:** Alright, let's get into exactly how PEST-Hub works.

The problem we're solving is that current pest identification methods are slow, inconvenient, and limited to fixed databases, which prevents timely action when unfamiliar pests appear. Step three, if the pest is recognized from our curated database of common agricultural pests, the user gets an immediate match with full details. But if the pest is not in our database, and this is where we truly innovate, our adaptive AI dynamically generates a complete pest profile on the spot.

### SLIDE 19 (Solution - Technology)

**GAVIN:** Under the hood, our technology stack is built for reliability and scale. Our backend runs on Flask, handling API requests and orchestrating our AI pipeline. And our AI engine is Google Gemini 2.0 Flash, one of the most capable multimodal AI models available today.

Pretrained models like Gemini are built on massive datasets, which means they understand a far broader range of pests than any custom model we could train ourselves. They require no training infrastructure, which keeps our costs low. They receive regular updates and improvements from Google, which means our system gets better automatically. And critically, they're multimodal, meaning they understand both the image and the agricultural context simultaneously, which produces far more accurate and useful results than simple image pattern matching.
