### SLIDE 5 (Overview)

**GAVIN:** PEST-Hub is an AI-powered mobile application that lets anyone identify agricultural pests instantly using just their smartphone camera. But it's more than a simple identification app. What sets us apart is that PEST-Hub doesn't just recognize pests from a fixed list. It uses adaptive artificial intelligence to identify any pest, including species never documented in our system, and generates a complete information profile on the spot, covering what the pest is, what damage it causes, and exactly how to treat and prevent it with both organic and chemical options.

We built PEST-Hub around four core components. First, instant AI pest detection, where our system identifies a pest from a single photo in seconds with a confidence score. Second, adaptive AI generation, our key innovation, where the system creates full pest profiles from scratch for species it's never encountered, instead of returning "unknown" like every other tool. Third, dual treatment recommendations, providing both organic and chemical paths for every pest. And fourth, mobile-first, field-ready design, built for someone standing in a field, not sitting at a desk.

### SLIDE 6 (Business Model)

**ZAIN:** Our business model is built on three revenue streams. The foundation is a freemium model. The basic app is completely free, any farmer can download it, scan a pest, and get an identification with treatment recommendations at no cost. We want zero barriers to entry. Our premium tier targets commercial farming operations with advanced features like batch scanning, historical pest tracking, and priority AI processing.

Beyond subscriptions, we partner with agricultural extension programs and government agencies who already serve millions of farmers. They license PEST-Hub as the pest identification tool they deploy to their networks. And our third stream is B2B licensing of our adaptive AI engine to existing ag-tech companies that need pest detection capabilities but don't have them. Three diversified revenue streams that scale naturally with adoption.

**ZAIN:** Now why does this matter so urgently? According to the Food and Agriculture Organization, up to forty percent of global crop production is lost every year to pests and diseases. That's over two hundred twenty billion dollars in annual crop loss worldwide. But the real crisis isn't that pests exist, it's that when they can't be identified quickly, everything cascades. Infestations spread exponentially. Farmers apply the wrong treatments out of desperation, wasting money and worsening damage. And every day of delay compounds the financial loss.

The barriers are enormous. There's limited access to agricultural experts in rural areas. Misidentification leads to ineffective or harmful treatments. Small farmers can't afford professional diagnostic services. And climate change is shifting pest populations into new regions where farmers have zero experience dealing with them.
